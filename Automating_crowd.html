What is open source?

Let's start with a standard definition
ixek

In the context of open source software

If software is open source then its source (code) is freely available to the world.

Meaning that it can be used, remix, licensed, and distributed at will

The term open [source] can be extended to many other things: hardware, design, scholarship

Meet grandma Mae

What is going to happen when she is not around?

ixek

What can she do?

  • Sell the recipe and make £££
  • Start her own business (every employee has to sign an NDA)
  • Share her recipe with the world
ixek

She has decided to open source her recipe

But she has added the following clauses:

  • People can use the recipe as they please (use, remix, modify)
  • She has to be given credit (using original grandma Maes recipe)
  • It can be distributed through any means (blog, email, cookbook)
ixek

What about a new feature?

ixek

How does this happen?

  1. A user suggest the feature (chocolate chips)
  2. The maintainer (grandma Mae) receives the request for approval
  3. The maintainer tests the feature
  4. The maintainer incorporates the feature if the tests are good
  5. Or not if she believes this is not the right thing to do (i.e. add nuts and allergic people cannot eat her cookies)


And the cookies recipe becomes an open source project driven by the community

ixek

With this increased responsibility grandma Mae has less time to bake
