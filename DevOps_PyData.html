What DevOps is not:

  • A replacement for operations as a whole
  • Not just tools
  • Not just culture
ixek
DevOps is the practice of operations and development engineers participating together in the entire service lifecycle, from design through the development process to production support.
https://theagileadmin.com/what-is-devops/
ixek
It is much more about collaboration and good enough practices to make your work more robust and efficient.

Than about a set of tools and technologies.
ixek

The three main practice areas are:

  • Infrastructure automation: systems, configs, and deployments as code
  • Continuous delivery: build, test, deploy
  • Site Reliability Engineering: monitoring, orchestration
ixek

So why should I (the data scientist) care?

If you answer yes to any of these:

  • Do your products reach the customers?
  • Are you generating new code/features/products?
  • Do you often need infra/engineering to take your models into production?
  • Are you the sole person responsible?
